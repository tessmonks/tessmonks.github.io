About Me

After graduating from the University of Richmond with a Bachelor's in Latin, Ancient Greek, Linguistics, and Economics, I enrolled in Harvard University's graduate program in Linguistics.

My love of linguistics began when I spent my Junior year studying at the University of Oxford as a Visiting Student. I learned to understand typology and language change through ancient texts, which I now implement as the basis for my computational and experimental approaches to semantic change.

I am a member of the Meaning & Modality Lab, the co-coordinator of Harvard's Workshop in Indo-European and Historical Linguistics, and the Linguistics Department social media manager. In my free time, I am training for the Cape Cod Marathon and competed at the World Championships of Irish Dancing in 2017.

Research

My research areas fall at the intersection of language change, computational linguistics, experimental linguistics, and formal theoretical semantics. My current project examines the grammaticalization cline from distal demonstrative ('that' in English) to complementizer, definite article, copula, and third person pronoun.

Chinwag

I host a podcast on linguistics in our everyday lives. From ergative languages to the proliferation of the word 'cheugy,' I cover it all in 15 minute episodes to help non-linguists engage in linguistic thought. You can find the show on RSS, Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and Instagram.
