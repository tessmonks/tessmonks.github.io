About Me

After graduating from the University of Richmond with a Bachelor's in Latin, Ancient Greek, Linguistics, and Economics, I enrolled in Harvard University's graduate program in Linguistics. My love of linguistics began when I spent my Junior year studying at the University of Oxford as a Visiting Student. I learned to understand typology and language change through ancient texts, which I now implement as the basis for my computational and experimental approaches to semantic change. I am a member of the Meaning & Modality Lab, the co-coordinator of Harvard's Workshop in Indo-European and Historical Linguistics, and the Linguistics Department social media manager. In my free time, I am training for the Cape Cod Marathon and competed at the World Championships of Irish Dancing in 2017.
